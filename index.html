What I did

I trained as a mechanical engineer at ETH Zürich. I did projects in robotics with drones, on the mathematical proofs behind the acoustic radiation force and on machine learning on satellite images of coral reefs.

What I do

I am a product owner working with a sharp, fun team on computer-vision-based inspection of the trains for the Swiss Railways.

What I do as well

I now build with Claude small GitHub projects that automate things in my daily life that don't deserve my attention. I also reflect on data that I have been gathering in the past or in the present, and create projects that connect the sources and generate insights that I I was unaware before. I became aware about the data-privacy issues the open-source version of the API was broken. Still I use them, because I care about collect data about the world, and so I might as well start with myself.

The rest of the time (made free) you can find me in doing solo-sports (with friends) like running, bouldering or swimming.

On the weekends can also find me in an (European) city in a coffee reading magazines, at the cinema or appreciating the details of the surrondings. In Summer my favourite place are the Swiss Alps. Occasionally I am at the seaside and observe the marine life.

Where my roots are

LaTeX was my first encounter with some form of code. I started writing physics summaries. From the start I appreciated the rigour in the source and the clean typographic look as well in the output.

As my skills improved I was fascinated by the complex visuals that can be created by writing code. It was a clear choice at the time I received the first hand-written version of my mother's family tree, which has branches back 5 generation in California and Ticino, to translate it in a latex-version. Every now and then an unknown relative knocks on my grandfather's door to give and collect information. The tree keeps growing.
